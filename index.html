About Me

I am a computational fluid dynamics researcher working at the interface of hydrodynamic stability theory, mathematical modeling, and scientific computing. My research focuses on understanding instability mechanisms in complex fluid systems using spectral numerical methods and high-performance simulations. During my doctoral work at the Indian Institute of Technology Jodhpur, I investigated the stability characteristics of several classes of flow systems, including rotating channel flows, vibrating free-surface flows, vibrating channel flows, and flows through anisotropic and inhomogeneous porous media. This work led to several publications in journals such as Physics of Fluids and Computers & Fluids.

My research combines applied mathematics, numerical methods for partial differential equations, and computational techniques to analyze the dynamics of fluid systems relevant to engineering and the physical sciences. I develop and implement computational frameworks using MATLAB, Python, and C/C++, often leveraging high-performance computing environments to investigate modal and non-modal stability in complex flows.

More broadly, I am interested in applying mathematical modeling and computational simulation techniques to challenging problems in fluid mechanics and related engineering systems, including microfluidics, porous media transport, and instability phenomena in technologically relevant flows.
